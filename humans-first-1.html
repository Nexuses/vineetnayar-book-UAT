NOT ANTI-AI.

NOT A LECTURE.

Thirty sparks, not thirty rules — meant to be argued with, not memorised.

NOT THE END.

The beginning of a question worth keeping: what stays human when the machines get good?

Trust

Steady ground before speed

Trust is not certainty. It is the solid ground people stand on before the pace picks up, so they act on judgment instead of fear. Like an anchor that does not rush, but simply holds.

Empower

Belief, not permission

Empowering people is not handing them access and waiting for them to ask. It is believing they can think, decide and lead without being told, so they stop waiting for instructions and start owning what comes next.

Amplify

Make people more, not less

AI was never meant to do your thinking. It should clear the dull, repetitive work so there is more room for judgment, imagination and care, leaving people sharper rather than smaller. The tool serves the person, not the other way around.

Reimagine

Win a different game

Once a machine can copy the old way of winning, that way loses its value. The future belongs to people who ask better questions and decide what is worth building, choosing a game only a human can win.
